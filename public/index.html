Furever Pal

An AR app to help potential pet owners find the right pet to adopt

OVERVIEW

“Furever Pal” was a project developed in the course of User-Centered Design Interaction at University of Washington. The app allows users to find a adoptable pet they like, see how it would look in their homes and fit their lifestyles. It features AR (augmented reality) experience and Tinder-inspired swiping interaction to streamline the decision making process for users to choose the right pet.

TIME

2017/09 - 2017/12

ROLE

User Research
Usability Testing UX Design Iteration

USER RESEARCH

Problem Space

Only 1 out of 10 animals in shelters is adopted while 2.7 million dogs and cats are put to sleep each year. The purpose of our project is to improve communication between animal shelters and those interested in pet ownership. However, we discovered that the scope of our goal was too broad and general, therefore we narrowed our scope to focus on the adoption process.

Original Design Question

"How might we help improve communication between animal shelters and potential pet owners, so that the adoption process goes more smoothly?"

Research Methods

We conducted quantitative and qualitative research methods among our target users to better understand the adoption process from both animal shelters’ perspectives and pet owners’ perspectives. Survey: We designed a survey targeting current and potential pet owners and received 90 responses in a week Interview: We conducted interviews with three local animal shelters: Regional Animal Services of King County (RASKC), Emerald Pet City Rescue, and PAWS Contextual Inquiry: We conducted contextual inquiry at a pet adoption event hosted by Ginger’s Pet Rescue. We were able to witness both stakeholders’ sides - not only how employees and volunteers do their jobs, but also how potential adopters interact with the staff and pets

Research Findings

From the interviews:

From the survey results: 86.7% of pet owners who have adopted a pet found that the adoption process to be open and easy 81% of current pet owners felt that finding and choosing the right pet that fit their lifestyle were the most difficult part of the adoption process Current pet owners thought the interaction with the pet was important in the adoption process Detailed descriptions, pictures and videos of the pets are the most important adoption information

Based on our research findings, we found that the adoption process is already easy and smooth here in Seattle; however, we were intrigued that many people found the decision making process to be the most difficult part of the entire adoption process. We decided to refine our design question to focus on that specific aspect, which is choosing the right pet.

Refined Design Question

How might we help improve communication between animal shelters and potential pet owners, so that the decision making part of the adoption process is easier?

PERSONA

Primary users: Potential pet adopters We hope to introduce a solution for potential pet owners to discover and connect with pets that are best suited for their requirements and conditions.

Design Requirements 1. The solution should improve the discoverability of adoptable pets
2. The solution should help adopters find pets that are most suitable for them
3. The solution should make the decision process easier for adopters
4. The solution should provide an immersive experience helping potential adopters to emotionally connect with the pets

Secondary users: Staffs, fosters, and volunteers of animal shelters These people will use our solution to manage and maintain useful information of pets in their shelters. These pieces of information are necessary to help adopters choose the right pet.

Design Requirements 1. The solution should provide a convenient mechanism for managing pet information
2. The solution should help produce better marketing content of adoptable pets
3. The solution should improve animal shelters’ reach to more potential pet adopters

IDEATION

Our team conducted an ideation session and sketched 20+ ideas to meet our users’ needs based on our research findings.

Keeping the primary use case “finding the right pet” in mind, we critique and filter the sketches, then decided to combine two of the most promising ideas - Tinder-inspired pet finding app with augmented reality (AR) to streamline the decision process - to create our design solution prototype. This combination would create the strongest product to tackle our design question.

Our design solution “Furever Pal” is an easy-to-use and intuitive app for users to efficiently find a pet they like and see how that pet would look in their homes and fit their lifestyles.

DESIGN

Our designer chose to use warm colors and Sans serif fonts to create a light, refreshing and engaging app design. The app should also provide an effortless, intuitive and smooth user experience.

Constraints

It is hard to present immersive AR experience in the mockup and the prototype Low-fidelity prototypes or paper prototypes cannot highlight the efficiency of the swiping mechanism Therefore, our team decided to build a high-fidelity mockup for prototyping with a creative way to showcase the AR feature for our product.

PROTOTYPE

How Our Prototype Supports The Design Requirements

We derived design requirements from our research findings: intuitive design that improves pet discoverability, help people find a suitable pet, provide an immersive experience, and help make the decision process easier. Users will be able to use our design mockup to complete the following use cases:
1. Find a pet by swiping left or right
2. Use AR to get a better sense of the pet
3. Reserve a meet & greet with the pet

USABILITY TESTING

We conducted 5 usability testings and get feedbacks from on our key questions to improve our design solution. The folowing are the findings:

What Worked 4/5 users thought our app makes finding an adoptable pet more efficient and make discoverability better 4/5 users thought the survey questions helped choose and filter the most suitable pets All 5 users liked the AR feature

What Didn't Work 4/5 users accidentally swiped left on a pet and wished they could go back and learn more about the pet before deciding to pass or like. 4/5 users didn’t know that to learn more information about the pet they needed to swipe up. They tried to “tap” instead 3/5 users suggested including more information on the meet and greet confirmation pop-up 2/5 users were not tech-savvy and didn’t know the term “AR”

ITERATION

Improved the instruction overlay: simplified the text and updated the symbols “Go Back” functionality: add a button to allow users to go back to a pet they may have accidentally swiped left on Rebranded AR Feature: change the text of the AR button for users to understand what this function does Confirmation Pop-Up: provide name of animal shelter in addition to the date and time of the appointment Enhanced pet information with multimedia content: add carousel design to allow users to discover more pictures and videos Survey design: add more choices to pet activity level

FUTURE DIRECTION

REFLECTION

As the first project in the HCDE master program, we went through a full research process during this project. We am are lucky to have have a cross-functional team consists with a clear division of work, and we gained different field knowledge and feedbacks from each other.

I also learned how to scope down our ideas into a complete solution since we started our project with huge ambitions and too broad of a goal. We could have a more specific focus for a more accurate research direction. Also, we would like to recruit real target users for usability testings to validate our design.

Bookme

http://bookme2.herokuapp.com/

OVERVIEW

“BookeMe” is a award-winning project created in cooperation with three developers and a designer in the 2017 Booking.com Taipai hackathon.
“BookMe” aims to simplify the decision making process by providing personalized travel recommendations with the best offers on Booking.com. The app applies image recognition technique to the content users interacted with on Instagram, and analyzes their preference to recommend travel spots accurately.

TIME

2017/03

ROLE

User Research
Interaction Design

CONSTRAINTS

Our team must start the project with a blank slate With only 48 hours to finish the project and the final presentation, the time for user research, design iteration and preparing for final presentation is extreme limited

USER RESEARCH

User Journey

We aimed to understand the online travel booking experience starting from users’ first step of decision making within 2 hours. At the beginning, We created a user journey of a travel shopper to help us visualize the areas we needed to target for our research.

Then, we conducted 5 semi-structured interviews with people who have booked a trip online. Below are the main findings:

Users want to determine where they should go efficiently with all the offers when they are planning a trip Users want more personalized travel suggestions based on their preference instead of recommendations based on travel history on their Booking.com homepage Users tend to make travel decisions according to interaction on social network and friends’ suggestion (52% of users user social media to plan a trip) Users want to get notified when the best offers appears of their desirable travel spots

User Stories

After analyzing and organizing all the information, the detailed user stories were formulated. The following are the main requirements of the app in the user stories:

Then, we conducted 5 semi-structured interviews with people who have booked a trip online. Below are the main findings:

Users can log in with their social media account in the product Users can get personalized travel suggestions based on a more precise preference setting instead of existing filters Users’s interaction with their friends on social media can help them make travel decisions easier Users can get notified when the best offers appears of their desirable travel spots

We decided to build an app “Bookme” that reverse the way users used to plan a trip. A user tends to decide where to travel based on friends’ recommendation on trip planner websites, but the biggest problem is that the results aren’t personalized. Therefore, we start the first step from analyzing user preferences more accurately to recommend places and best offers for a better planning experience.

DESIGN

Interaction Design

Based on the users stories, I first made the flowchart and then the wireframes to communicate and confirm the flow and the key features of the app.

Visual Design

The design intends to create a delightful feeling for travel shoppers to experience the new flow of planning a trip. The interaction should be smooth and effortless because it's suppose to lubricate the current booking process.

Development

We decided to apply image recognition techniques to the Instagram content users interacted with and analyze them with Google Cloud Platform API. The comprehensive results can analyze users’ preference more accurately and predictively for recommendations, and can optimize through machine learning.

PROTOTYPE

The engineers hand-coded a digital prototype in 5 hours to showcase our ideas and communicate the transition and interaction in the app.

FINAL IMPLEMENTATION

Booking.com aims to provide hotels, homes and everything in between. If Booking.com exceeds your expectation at the moment you open the room door, Bookme find the exact expectation for you!

FUTURE DIRECTION

Our team are positive about more business development about “Bookme”: For B2C : we want to link more current users and invite potential users from Boking.com For B2B: the recommendation results and user preference data can help related travel industries to understand user needs and create more accurate ads

REFLECTION

The engineers and product managers from Booking.com found our idea and prototype to be highly valuable. We received comments that this application addressed a well-known problem and used image recognition techniques appropriately to solve the problem, even from business perspective.

The hackathon was a challenging and insightful two-day training. I have gained hands-on experience about the collaboration process of development within a limited time, and how to make rapid progress in a small team.

Backme Redesign

backme.tw

OVERVIEW

First launch in 2014, Backme is a payment application developed by Backer-Founder. By releasing customized APIs to easily embed into a website, users can create a crowdfunding campaign or an e-commerce website without the limitation of a crowdfunding platform. Project creators can also manage their campaign, user data, and transaction records on the admin panel. Backme has assisted over 700 projects to go online, with a total cash flow of $100 million USD and 300,000+ transactions.

As the product manager, I conducted user research and usability testings to propose usability insights and new feature recommendations. I coordinated with UI designers and developers to redesign the admin panel and the check out process, eventually increased conversion by 28% and reduced bounce rate by 35%.

TIME

2016/02-2017-08

ROLE

Product Manager

USER RESEARCH

I conducted 5 interviews and contextual inquiries with three types of users: clients & consumers (external), project managers (internal) and developers / designers. Contextual inquiries complement the interview answers, and helped me define users’ need when I observe their behavior using Backme.

User Stories

For clients, they want a clear visual hierarchy to help them find necessary informations to deal with customer service on the admin panel For project managers, they want to have access to real-time sales data from different sources (ideally visualized), so that they can calculate conversion rate to adjust marketing strategies For developers and designers, the application lacks a systematic design guideline, which makes it hard to develop and iterate efficiently

Define Problems

Clients cannot find necessary project information because the navigation and visual hierarchy lack simplicity and clarity. Furthermore, the search bar was nor presented unless users scroll to the right Project managers need to spend a lot of time dealing with customer services and explaining how to use the product for project creators Project managers want to see the real-time conversion data in a clear form like other analytical tools, so they can adjust marketing strategies more efficiently Project managers can hardly categorize information from mutual projects without a tag / label system in the database Project managers point out that the high bounce rate (83%) at the check out page should be optimized The app needs a design guideline so designers and developers can implement new designs with a clear principle

DESIGN

Based on the users stories, I prioritized the tasks and created wireframes with the designer together to communicate the features. As the product manager, I invited all the stakeholders to monthly meetings to reach agreement on key features and the design decision.

Task 1: Redesign the Project Dashboard

We redesigned the project dashboard to refine the navigation and visual hierarchy of the original project dashboard and provide project information more efficiently.

Information Architecture

We held a meeting with all the stakeholders involved to reorganized the information architecture of the Project Dashboard to ensure users can easily discover all the necessary project information they need.

We decided to design two versions that users can switch between:
Grid view: implements card style design with a more responsive display for different screen sizes. It present project image with brief introduction, sales and backer number List view: displays more projects in a limited space. It presents only the project name, sales number and backer number

We also added tags and labels as part of the project information, so users can categorized similar projects. We carefully regulate the operating behavior of each card, eliminating distracting elements so that the users can easily find the key gadgets.

Wireframes

The designer and I created wireframes to conceptualize the ideas and communicate with developers. Also, I present wireframes to project managers to ensure the space allocation and prioritization of content meet their needs.

Task 2: Redesign the Data Analysis Dashboard

One of the important tasks for project managers is to keep track of conversion rate to optimize marketing strategies. Originally, they analyze website traffic and conversions by comparing the user data on Google Analytics with the sales number on Backme to calculate conversion rate manually.

However, Backme actually keeps track of the Google UTM parameters code for individual link. Therefore, our goal is to redesign the data display on the Data Analysis Dashboard, which helps project managers evaluate real-time conversion rate efficiently.

To present a clear interface of data analysis, we decided to visualize the main indicators of user behaviors for the “Marketing Campaign Analysis” chart. The designer created an intuitive UI that condensed huge amounts of data into charts, which can also display real-time data responsively when users hover. Use spline chart to display the traffic from different marketing campaign sources Use bar chart to present the CTR (Click-through rate) rankings of individual marketing campaign

We also created the new feature of “ROI calculator” (Return On Investment) by importing Google Analytics tracking data and calculate on Backme. Users can input the cost for each advertising campaign to calculate the conversion rate automatically.

Task 3: Optimize the Checkout Process

As an embedded online payment tool, the user goals of our product are:
1. For clients, they want to ensure the conversion rate at checkout - the last step of the online shopping process
2. For end users (customers), they want to buy products online with a easy to understand, simple, and secure checkout experience

Before optimizing, Backme has a 83% bounce rate in the check out process. We redesigned several key points to optimize users’ payment experience as simple and intuitive as possible. One month after the design is implemented, we successfully increased conversion rates by 28% and reduced bounce rate by 35%.

Chunking Checkout Steps

We improved the design by chunking the checkout process into 3 steps so users feel simple and easy. The 3 step design is suggested by a payment study conducted by Shopify. By grouping relevant information together in chunks, and putting it in a logical order, Backme allows users to focus on one task at a time. Confirm Order → Shipping Information → Payment

Web Form Filling Experience

Filling the form is considered the final step to complete checkout. Users should be able to complete forms quickly and without confusion.

We aim to make our users feel that registration is a value-added benefit, even though it is mandatory. Therefore, Backme only request necessary information and asks to create an account in order to track the order progress at the order confirmation stage.

The followings are some tips of redesigning our web form filling experience: Only ask for required information because every extra field will affect its conversion rate Order the form logically from the user’s perspective, and group related fields together Use drop down menu for a list of options (e.g. Country Lists) Provide visual feedback on correct information and adaptive error message when users enter information incorrectly

UI DESIGN GUIDELINE

The designer expanded the original design guideline with more UI components which provided a clear principle for developing upcoming new features. The updated design guidelines provide helpful advice on how to achieve a platform-specific design principle for Backme.

REFLECTION

I have participated in the product development on both stakeholder’s side - as the product manager and the user (project manager) - for almost two years. We ran sprints internally with different beta version then released for our clients in the crowdfunding and e-commerce industry. We used these feedbacks to identify key functionalities, prioritize tasks and keep refining the design for better user experience.

I have gained practical experience on how to make rapid progress in a cross-functional team, and keep iterating the product design and strategy through repetitive testing, user data and customer validation.

My team at Backer-Founder was responsible for the Ticwatch Taiwan pre-order campaign. We analyzed user insights through research to develop holistic marketing and design strategies, and pushed the sales number over 1 million USD. It is ranked as the most funded crowdfunding/pre-order project in Taiwan in 2017.

TIME

2016/12 - 2017/08

ROLE

Project Manager

At the beginning, our client provided us the survey results about users’ profile and buying behavior in US and China market. We discovered that users in different countries tend to have distinct preferences on features and style in terms of wearable products. Therefore, we decided to conduct a quantitive research to clarify the user profile in Taiwan market.

Survey

We designed three types of surveys by targeting three different potential user groups with Facebook ads:
1. Users who are interested in wearable devices
2. Users who are familiar with other smartwatch brands
3. Tech-savvy users and early adopters

We want to make sure that users will be provided with specifically customized content in tune with their respective interests and expectations.

More than 5,000+ survey responses (including e-mail lists) were collected within a month, which helped us identify the most compelling features and buying incentives to our target users, define a clearer potential user profile, and further scope the design focus of our website and advertising.

Product images are a powerful way to capture the user’s attention and to differentiate a product. WEBSITE DESIGN

Ticwatch Pre-order Website: www.ticwatch2.com.tw

To build the pre-order website with localized design and customized content based on our user research, we discard copying the style from the global website and Kickstarter page. However, we still implement consistent branding across all channels so that users recognize the brand and the product.

I coordinated with designers and developers to create a engaging digital experience for the website. It also has a responsive design that adapts to look perfect on all of the different devices.

Information Architecture & Content Strategy

We aim to design a website with a clear information architecture and hierarchy which allow users to discover the information they expect easily. We come up with the main sections of the website:
1. List the achievements on Kickstarter and media quotes for brand awareness and enhance users’ confidence toward crowdfunding products
2. Demonstrate product highlights with interactive design
3. Contextualize the product, allowing users to understand the features they care about
4. Provide product specification to ensure users have all the detailed information before purchase
5. Improve SEO (Search Engine Optimization) with accurate keywords in HTML meta tags to affect the online visibility of our website

Visual Hierarchy

The website has been designed with different color sections that creates a neat hierarchy of content and helps users to navigate through the page with a nice rhythm. We also focus on the following design details:
Use three levels of typographic hierarchy to organize each section Design buttons consistently. Users associate a particular element’s shape with button functionality Create a sticky navigation bar helps to drive traffic to the shopping cart with Call-To-Action Buttons that remains on top of the screen at all times Bring visual prominence to the Call-To-Action Buttons with contrasting colors

Animation

We incorporate animation as a functional element to enhance user experience. For example, To create an intuitive, user-friendly checkout process, we provide visual feedback on user action in the shopping cart, which helps inform users about the result of their operation.

Animal Savior

http://bookme2.herokuapp.com/

OVERVIEW

Animal Savior is a 2017 Red Dot Award winner in Product Design, and also the first ever crowdfunding project of sex toy in Taiwan. It is known for its groundbreaking animal outlines, delicate texture as well as intuitive control. My team at Backer-Founder assisted with the overall marketing strategies and campaign page design, achieving the funding target of $80,000 USD with 1,546 supporters in 45 days, which was 350% of the expected target.

STRATEGY PLANNING

Male consumers accounted for more than 70% of the sex toy market in Taiwan. However, the characteristics of “Animal Savior” gave a sense of cuteness, safety, trustworthiness as well as real affordability, which allowed us to set its target users as females who were buying sex toys for the first time.

This strategy was applied to our copywriting, website design, video and other promotional materials in order to create a consistency brand identity and connect with first-time female buyers.

USER RESEARCH

We decided to conduct pretotyping to validate our product strategy and collect feedbacks from female consumers when facing such sensitive product.

Survey

We designed the survey as a psychological test about “Which Cute Animal Fits You The Best?” to introduce the five product characters to potential users. The survey result will pair the user’s personality with one of the animals, hoping to arouse their interest and break the stereotype of sex toys.

More than 2,000+ survey responses (including e-mail lists) were collected within three weeks, which helped us identify the most popular product feature to our target users, define a clearer demographic profile, and further validate the design and copywriting style of the entire campaign.

Research Findings

73% of our survey respondents have’t purchased sex toys before. The first-time buying incentive will be the affordable price (under $30 USD) of the product The age range of potential users are 18-25 years old young female who enjoys sports, makeup and online shopping For potential users, the most appealing product feature is the cute animal outline that even fits in girls’ makeup bag

CAMPAIGN MATERIAL DESIGN

Color is one of the most recognizable aspect of our product. The designer use warm and bright colors which correlate with the products to create an delightful brand identity and memorable visual impression for young female demographic. The visual style we defined also differentiated our product from other competitors on the market.

Commercial Photography

We mapped out two sets of commercial photography that aligned to our user profile:
1. Contextualize the product
We gave the product characteristic with storytelling by putting the animals in their living environments to emphasize the adorable product outline
2. “What’s in my bag” image series
Since each animal corresponds to a specific type of female consumers, we put the product together with different types of accessories that are commonly found in a girl’s purse to resonate with users. The pictures also give users a better sense about who is likely to use the product and which style to choose from

Motion Graphic Design

Based on previous experiences, videos are one of the most widespread crowdfunding campaign methods. We created a motion graphic with coherent colors which is consistent with the brand design guideline, and also made a strong visual impression on viewers.

The animator set the living environment of each animal as a metaphor for body parts. The adorable illustrations elaborated the fact that enjoying solo sex is natural. So goes the product’s saying, “Girls also have the right to love themselves accompanied by the cute animals.”

WEBSITE DESIGN

One-Page Responsive Web Design

Our team proposed to create a one-page responsive webpage instead of using the crowdfunding platform template without a mobile version. We believed a well-designed webpage will better present the product feature and provide users an immersive browsing experience.

This is the first ever crowdfunding campaign on the uDesign platform presented with one single landing page. The average site visit duration reached 8 minutes with over 48,000 visitors (average site visit duration in the e-commerce industry is 1.5 minutes.) The extraordinary sales results and positive feedback have proved that website design strongly affects how a product is perceived.

Visual Design

The web design also followed the brand identity and color guideline set previously to create a delightful, fresh and warm feeling instead of sexy. We also make sure to put each design element in the interface that has a corresponding user story.

First screen:
We caught users’ attention with the carousel design of full-sized contextualized product images. The vivid visual impression will lead them to discover more information on the website Product introduction page:
Each animal had its own introduction webpage which users can click through the sticky navigation bar. The introduction pages creates a complete context and story along with the commercial photography, allowing users to understand the different style and features they care about Warning signs:
We redesigned the pop-up window with animation of the animals so users would be less resistant towards warning signs. The design has drastically lowered the bounce rate to 57% when users entered the website

Content Strategy

Besides visual design, we also gave the product a lively image through copywriting. We carefully wrote in our users’ tone to reflect their needs and feelings, and hope that they would view Animal Savior in a comfortable, non-pornographic light.

The followings are the key points of our website content: Emphasize the harmless appearance of small animals:
“Animal Savior” is a little gadget that makes you happy rather than an actual sex toy. It is equivalent to your makeup gadgets – its size fits easily into your purse Provide answer to users’ hesitation - why would I need a sex toy?
“Just a gentle touch, which doesn’t have to be the touch of your lover – can make you feel loved and cared for,” breaking the stereotype that sex toys must always be sexy and steamy. Put the product into context with style suggestions:
We tailored a unique characteristic for each animal, allowing users to relate and choose the styles they like the most Certified material and secure experience:
The product used extremely soft, FDA approved medical silicone to provide a gentle vibration and pleasant non-invasive experience especially for first-time users.

CAMPAIGN PROMOTION

Organic Reach & Advertising
